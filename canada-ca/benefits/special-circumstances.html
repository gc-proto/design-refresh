Special circumstances


Multiple births

The number of weeks of maternity or parental benefits you are entitled to receive doesn't change if you have a multiple birth (twins, triplets, etc.) or if you adopt more than one child at the same time.

Sickness and bedrest during pregnancy

Although pregnancy and childbirth aren't considered to be illnesses, complications with respect to either may be. As long as eligibility conditions are met, a person may receive EI sickness benefits before the birth of a child and transition to maternity and then parental benefits afterwards.

Health complications for your baby

You may be eligible for other benefits, such as caregiving benefits if your child becomes critically ill. You must meet the eligibility requirements for each benefit.

We encourage you to contact Service Canada directly to speak with an agent who can give you advice for your specific circumstances.

Miscarriage, termination or stillbirth

Maternity leave is for health-related needs surrounding childbirth. If your pregnancy ends in miscarriage, termination or stillbirth after the 19th full week of pregnancy, you are still eligible for maternity benefits.

If your pregnancy ends before the 19th week, you may take apply for EI sickness benefits.

Parental leave doesn't apply after a miscarriage, termination or stillbirth.

Surrogacy Surrogacy

EI maternity benefits are offered to biological mothers, including surrogate mothers, who can't work because they are pregnant or have recently given birth.

Death of a child

If you are consulting this section following the death of a child, please accept our condolences. We know that this is a very difficult time for you.

Payment of maternity benefits isn't affected by the death of a child. Maternity benefits are granted to help you recover following pregnancy and childbirth.

Payment of parental benefits ends following the death of a child. If the death occurs while parental benefits are being paid, parents cease to be eligible for the benefits as of the week following the death of the child.

You must inform us of your child’s date of death as soon as possible so that we can update your file and make any necessary adjustments.

Blended families sharing parental leave

Parental benefits can be shared with up to 4 legally recognized parents. The number of weeks of parental benefits you are entitled to receive doesn't change.

Non-consecutive parental leave

If you started a new Employment Insurance (EI) claim within the last 52 weeks and there are still weeks payable on that claim, we will automatically reactivate (renew) your existing claim when you submit your application.

In some cases, it may be to your advantage to cancel or end your old claim and start a new claim, because this may increase the amount of your benefits or the length of your benefit period.

You must decide whether or not to cancel or renew a claim based on your own personal situation.

It is important to consider:

  • if your claim is reactivated and you work after the start of that claim, you may be able to establish a new claim when your existing claim runs out
  • in order to establish a new claim you must have enough insurable hours and meet the qualifying conditions for a new claim
  • if a new claim is established instead of reactivating your existing claim, the remaining weeks payable on the existing claim will be lost
  • a one-week unpaid waiting period must be served on a new claim before you are entitled to receive payment
