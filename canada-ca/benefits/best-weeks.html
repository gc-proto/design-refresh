3. How much you could receive

The amount could vary between maternity and parental benefits. We can't tell exactly how much you will receive until your application is processed.

Maternity benefits

For most people, EI maternity benefits are 55% of your average insurable weekly earnings, up to a maximum amount. ("Insurable" means you paid EI premiums on them.) In 2018, the maximum amount is $547 a week.

Parental benefts

EI parental benefits depend on the payment option you choose:

Standard parental benefits last up to 35 weeks and are paid at a weekly rate of 55% of your average insurable weekly earnings, up to a maximum amount. In 2018, the maximum amount is $547 a week.

Extended parental benefits last up 61 weeks and are paid at a weekly rate of 33% of your average weekly insurable earnings, up to a maximum amount. In 2018, the maximum amount is $328 per week. This amount can be increased if you are eligible to receive the Family Supplement.

Employer-sponsored income top-ups

Some employers may offer you an income top-up when you're on parental leave. This top-up pays a portion of your regular salary and supplements your EI or Quebec Parental Insurance Plan benefits.

If your employer offers a top-up, find out:

  • how much you'll get
  • how long the top-up will last
  • if you’ll be required to return to work by a certain time and remain at your job for a minimum period of time

EI Family Supplement

If we determine that your net family income is $25,921 or less per year, that you have children, and that you or your spouse receives the Canada Child Benefit, you may be eligible to receive the Family Supplement. It would be applied automatically by Service Canada. No action is required from you.

The amount of Family Supplement you receive depends on:

  • your net family income (up to the $25,921 yearly maximum); and
  • the number of children in your family, and their ages.
