When you must open (register for) a GST/HST account

You must register for a GST/HST account if both situations apply:

  • you make taxable sales, leases, or other supplies in Canada (unless your only taxable supplies are sales of real property that are not in the course of a business)
  • you are not a small supplier

Find out if you must register by answering a few questions.

Self-employed taxi driver or commercial ride-sharing driver

If you are a self-employed taxi driver or commercial ride-sharing driver you must register for a GST/HST account even if you are a small supplier. Your effective date of registration is the day you start supplying taxable passenger transportation services. For more information, see GST/HST information for taxi operators and commercial ride-sharing drivers.

Non-resident

If you are a non-resident, see Non-resident GST/HST registration.

Financial institution

If you are a selected listed financial institution, see GST/HST Notice 265, GST/HST Registration for Listed Financial Institutions (including Selected Listed Financial Institutions).

Voluntary registration

Sometimes there are reasons that you might decide to register for a GST/HST account even though you are not required to.

Decide if you want to register voluntarily.
