Apply

You should apply as soon as possible after you stop working. You can't apply in advance. If you delay filing your claim by more than four weeks after your last day of work, you may lose benefits.

When to apply: Maternity benefits

Maternity benefits

You can choose to stop working and apply for maternity benefits before you give birth. You can start receiving benefits as early as 12 weeks before your due date. You can't receive EI maternity benefits more than 17 weeks after the week you actually gave birth.

If you must stop working for health or other reasons, see Special circumstances.

When to apply: Parental benefits

Parental benefits

You can apply after the baby is born or placed with you for the purpose of adoption, and you have stopped working. You do not need to apply again if you applied as part of your maternity benefits application.

You can only receive these benefits within a specific benefit period:

  • Standard parental benefits within 52 weeks after the child is born or placed with you for the purpose of adoption
  • Extended parental benefits within 78 weeks after the child is born or placed with you for the purpose of adoption

Application process


1. Choose a benefit option

2. Complete the online application

3. Provide your Record of Employment

4. Notify us of actual date of birth or adoption

5. Wait for your access code

6. Review your application status

Choose between standard and extended parental benefits

Select which parental benefit option you wish to claim:

  • Standard parental (up to 35 weeks, up to $547 per week)
  • Extended parental (up to 61 weeks, up to $328 per week)

The choice you make applies for the entire benefit period. You can't change between standard and extended once parental benefits have been paid.

Sharing benefits

When sharing benefits, parents must choose the same option.

The total number of weeks available for sharing is 40 for the standard option and 69 for the extended option.

However, no one parent can receive more than 35 weeks of standard or 61 weeks of extended parental benefits.

Complete the online application

Gather your personal information

Make sure you have all the information listed below to complete your application:

  • the name(s) and address(es) of your employer(s) for the last 52 weeks
  • your detailed explanation of the facts, if you quit or have been dismissed from any job in the last 52 weeks
  • the dates (Sunday to Saturday) and earnings for each of your highest paid weeks of insurable earnings in the last 52 weeks or since the start of your last EI claim, whichever is the shorter period
  • your full mailing address (and your home address, if they aren't the same)
  • your social insurance number (SIN)
  • the SIN of the other parent(s) if you plan to share the benefits
  • your mother's maiden name
  • your banking information, including financial institution, branch (transit) number and your account number, to sign up for direct deposit
  • the expected or actual date of birth
  • in the case of adoption: the date your child was placed with you and the full name and address of the agency handling the adoption

Apply online now

Provide your Record of Employment

Employers issue a Record of Employment (ROE) to provide information on your work history. Service Canada uses the information to determine:

  • whether you are eligible to receive Employment Insurance (EI) benefits
  • what the benefit amount will be

You can visit My Service Canada Account to view ROEs that have been issued to you by past and current employers.

Electronic ROEs

Electronic ROEs are sent directly to Service Canada from your employer. You don't need to request copies of electronic ROEs from your employer and you don't need to provide copies to Service Canada.

Paper ROEs

If your employer issues ROEs in paper format, you must request copies of all ROEs issued to you during the last 52 weeks. You must provide them to Service Canada as soon as possible after you submit your application for EI benefits.

You must mail the ROEs received from your employer(s) to Service Canada or drop them off in person at a Service Canada Centre.

The mailing address is on the "Information and Confirmation" page once you submit your online application for EI benefits.


Notify us of actual date of birth or adoption

When the actual date of birth is different from the expected date of birth, you must let us know the child's actual date of birth as soon as possible. You can:

  • call 1-800-206-7218 (TTY: 1-800-529-3742)
  • visit a Service Canada Centre

In the case of an adoption, provide the date of the child's placement with you, and the name and address of the adoption authority.

Wait for your access code

Service Canada will mail you a benefit statement once your application is complete. This statement will include a 4-digit access code. You need this code and your SIN to inquire about your application. Receiving the EI benefit statement doesn't mean that Service Canada has made a decision about your claim.

Review your application status

To check the progress of your application, you can:

  • log into My Service Canada Account
  • contact Service Canada

If you don't have an account, you can register for one once you receive your access code.
