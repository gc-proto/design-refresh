Eligibility

The information below should be used as a guideline. You’re encouraged to apply for benefits and allow a Service Canada representative to determine if you’re eligible.

You’ll need to demonstrate that:

  • you’re pregnant, or have recently given birth (when requesting maternity benefits)
  • you're a parent caring for your newborn or newly adopted child (when requesting parental benefits)
  • your regular weekly earnings from work have decreased by more than 40% for at least one week
  • you accumulated 600 hours of insurable earnings in the 52 weeks before the start of your claim or since the start of your last claim, whichever is shorter (600 hours is equivalent to 15 weeks of work at 40 hours per week)
    • insurable earnings are any income you paid EI premiums on, including tips, bonuses, and commissions.

    If you’re self-employed

    If you're self-employed

    If you registered for access to EI special benefits for self-employed people and waited 12 months from the date of your confirmed registration, you may be eligible for these benefits. You must meet the eligibility criteria for the benefit you are applying for. You must also meet all of the following conditions:

    • the amount of time you spend on your business has decreased by more than 40% for at least one week because you’re pregnant or have recently given birth or you’re caring for a newborn or newly adopted child
    • you have earned a minimum amount of self-employed earnings during the calendar year before the year you submit your claim. For 2018, you need to have earned at least $6,947 in 2017

    If you’re outside of Canada or a non-citizen

    If you’re outside of Canada or a non-citizen

    If you’re outside of Canada temporarily or permanently, you may still be eligible for maternity and parental benefits during your absence.

    If you’re a non-citizen, you may be eligible as long as your social insurance number hasn’t expired. Contact Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada to find out how travel outside of Canada may impact you.

    Eligibility period for maternity benefits

    You can start receiving benefits as early as 12 weeks before your due date. You can't receive EI maternity benefits more than 17 weeks after the week you actually gave birth.

    Eligibility period for parental benefits

    • Standard parental benefits can be paid within a 52-week period after the week the child is born or placed with you for the purpose of adoption.
    • Extended parental benefits can be paid within a 78-week period after the week the child is born or placed with you for the purpose of adoption.

        • If sharing, each parent must select the same option when submitting their application. Once you start receiving benefits, you cannot change options.
