After you've applied

When payments start

You will receive the first payment about 28 days after you apply, if you are eligible and have submitted all required documents. If you are not eligible, we will notify you of the decision we made about your claim.

Waiting period

Before you start receiving benefits, there is one week for which you will not be paid. This is what we call the waiting period. The waiting period is like the deductible that you must pay for other types of insurance.

If you apply for maternity benefits and parental benefits together, your waiting period will only be served once.

If you are sharing benefits for the same child, only one parent will serve the waiting period. Please contact Service Canada to confirm which parent will serve the one-week waiting period.

When payments end

You can find specific end dates for your payments by logging into your My Service Canada Account.

Maternity benefits

You will stop receiving benefits if:

  • you have received 15 weeks of benefit payments
  • 17 weeks have passed since you gave birth
Standard parental benefits

You will stop receiving benefits if:

  • you have received all benefit payments you applied for, up to a maximum of 35 weeks
  • 52 weeks have passed since the child was born or adopted
  • you stop providing care for your child
  • you have received the maximum 105 weeks of benefit payments, if the the benefit is combined with other types of EI benefits
Extended parental benefits

You will stop receiving benefits if:

  • you have received all benefit payments you applied for, up to a maximum of 61 weeks
  • 78 weeks have passed since the child was born or adopted
  • you stop providing care for your child
  • you have received the maximum 105 weeks of benefit payments, if the the benefit is combined with other types of EI benefits

Check or update your account

My Service Canada Account allows you to view and update your EI benefits information in one place using a secure website. You can:

  • confirm any decision made about your benefits application
  • see when your payments start and end
  • sign up for direct deposit, so payments are deposited automatically into your bank account
  • view and update your personal information, including your mailing address, telephone number, and banking information for direct deposit
  • view your EI tax information slips
  • view all Records of Employment that your employers have submitted electronically in the last two years

How to register for My Service Canada Account

Before you register, you must have your four-digit EI access code (printed in the shaded area at the bottom of your benefit statement). You can then register for My Service Canada Account. It will take about 10 minutes to complete the registration process.

While receiving benefits

While you are receiving benefits, you must continue to be eligible. Please contact Service Canada if:

  • you stop providing care for your child
  • you start working or earn money. For more information on how earnings impact your benefits, visit Working while on claim

If you do not inform Service Canada of these changes, you risk being overpaid and having to repay benefits.

If your situation changes

You may be eligible for other benefits. This could include sickness benefits if you become ill yourself, or caregiving benefits if your child becomes critically ill. You must meet the eligibility requirements for each benefit.

We encourage you to contact Service Canada directly to speak with an agent who can give you advice for your specific circumstances.

If you disagree with the decision

You can request a reconsideration of the decision on your application for EI benefits. You must submit a request for reconsideration within 30 days of the date of the decision.
