3. How much you could receive

We can't tell exactly how much you will get until your application is processed, but you can get an estimate of how much you could receive and for how long. Some employers provide additional money while you're on maternity or parental leave. This is called a top-up. Check with your employer to see if this applies to you.

Estimate your payments

The amount you get depends on your average weekly insurable earnings. Insurable earnings are any income you paid EI premiums on, including tips, bonuses, and commissions.

Estimated results:

This is for estimation only based on what you entered above. We can't tell exactly how much you will receive until your application is processed.

  • If you apply on December 14, 2018, you should begin receiving payments around January 15, 2019.
  • Your maternity benefits are estimated at $514 per week.
  • Your 15 weeks of maternity benefits will end about March 29, 2019.
  • Your regular parental benefits will then begin. Those are estimated at $514 per week.
  • You can take up to 31 weeks of parental benefits, ending around November 1, 2019
  • Other parents have 4 weeks to share and should apply separately.
  • You may be eligible for additional money through the EI Family Supplement

If you took extended parental benefits:

  • Your parental benefits would begin around March 29, 2019. Those are estimated at $314 per week.
  • You'll get 65 weeks of parental benefits, ending around June 26, 2020
  • Other parents have 4 weeks to share and should apply separately.

If your family income is $25,921 or below

If we determine that your net family income is $25,921 or less per year, that you have children, and that you or your spouse receives the Canada Child Benefit, you may be eligible to receive the Family Supplement. It would be applied automatically by Service Canada. No action is required from you.

The amount of Family Supplement you receive depends on:

  • your net family income (up to the $25,921 yearly maximum); and
  • the number of children in your family, and their ages.
