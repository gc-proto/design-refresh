Estimate your benefits

How many weeks of benefits you get depends on what leave options you choose. How much money you get depends on your insurable earnings over the past 52 weeks, before taxes. ("Insurable" means income you paid EI premiums on, including tips, bonuses, and commissions)

If you earn more in some weeks than others, the calculation is based on the weeks you made the most, known as your best weeks.

How we calculate your benefit amounts

  • Maternity benefits are paid at a weekly benefit rate of 55% of your average weekly insurable earnings, up to a maximum amount of $547. You could receive up to 15 weeks of maternity benefits. You can start receiving benefits as early as 12 weeks before your due date or before the actual week you give birth. You cannot receive maternity benefits more than 17 weeks after the week you were expected to give birth or the week you actually gave birth, whichever is later.

  • Standard parental benefits are paid at a weekly benefit rate of 55% of your average weekly insurable earnings, up to a maximum amount of $547. You could receive up to 35 weeks of standard parental benefits. If parents share benefits, this increases to 40 weeks. One parent cannot receive more than 35 weeks. These benefits can be paid during the 52 weeks following the week of the the child’s birth or adoption placement date.

  • Extended parental benefits are paid at a weekly benefit rate of 33% of your average weekly insurable earnings, up to a maximum amount of $328. You could receive up to 61 weeks of extended parental benefits. If parents share benefits, this increases to 69 weeks. One parent cannot receive more than 61 weeks. These benefits can be paid during the 78 weeks following the week of the child’s birth or adoption placement date.

If your income varies from week to week

Your average weekly earnings are taken from the weeks where you earned the most, also known as best weeks, over the past year. The number of best weeks to use for the average depends on where you live. Enter your postal code to see how many best weeks to use in your estimate.

If your family income is $25,921 or below

If we determine that your net family income is $25,921 or less per year, that you have children, and that you or your spouse receives the Canada Child Benefit, you may be eligible to receive the Family Supplement. It would be applied automatically by Service Canada. No action is required from you.

The amount of Family Supplement you receive depends on:

  • your net family income (up to the $25,921 yearly maximum); and
  • the number of children in your family, and their ages.
