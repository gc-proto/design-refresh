Register for a GST/HST account

To start charging the GST/HST you must register for an account. A GST/HST account number is part of a business number. If you don’t have a business number yet, you’ll receive one when you register for your GST/HST account.

If you are not sure if you need a GST/HST account:

  • find out when you must register
  • decide if you want to register voluntarily

What you need before you start

Have all the following information with you when you register:

1. Effective date of registration
Mandatory registration

The effective date of registration can be different depending on the type of business you are registering.

When to register provides the small supplier threshold limits for different types of businesses. Find details on the effective date of registration in different scenarios.

Self-employed taxi driver or commercial ride-sharing driver

Your effective date of registration is the day you start supplying taxable passenger transportation services.

Voluntary registration

If you request your GST/HST account when you are still a small supplier, your effective date of registration is usually the date of your request. It can also be up to 30 days before that day.

2. Fiscal year-end for GST/HST purposes

Know your fiscal year

Your fiscal year end for GST/HST purposes can be either:

  • December 31
  • the same as your fiscal year-end for income tax purposes

3. Total annual revenue

To calculate your total annual revenue, include:

  • your revenues from taxable supplies, including supplies that are zero-rated
  • the revenues from taxable supplies of all your associates

Do not include revenues from:

  • exempt supplies
  • financial services
  • sales of capital property
  • goodwill from the sale of a business

New businesses

If you are just starting your business, you can give us an estimate of your income for the year.

4. Basic information

Personal information

If you are a business owner or third party requester, you must provide all of the following:

  • last name
  • social insurance number: SIN (for online application only)
  • date of birth (for online application only)
  • personal postal code (for online application only)

How we use your personal postal code (where you live)

We use your personal postal code to verify your identity. We check the postal code you enter against the postal code we have on file for you, not the one on file for your business.

If you have recently moved, you should check to make sure we have the right postal code on file for you by calling Individual Income Tax Enquiries.


Basic business information

You must provide the following information for your business:

  • business name
  • business number (if this business already has one)
  • type of business or organization (for example: sole proprietor, partnership, corporation, registered charity)
  • name and SIN of all owners
  • physical address
  • mailing address (if different from the physical address)
  • description of major business activity

How to register

In most cases, you can register for a GST/HST account online.

Online:
Business registration online
Note: businesses located in Quebec and non-resident businesses are not eligible for this online registration tool
By phone:
1-800-959-5525 Monday to Friday (except holidays) from 9 am to 6 pm
By mail or fax:
Complete Form RC1A, Business Number - GST/HST Account Information, and mail it to your tax centre
Quebec business:
Visit Revenu Québec

If you have already started charging the tax

If you have charged the tax on your sales more than 30 days before registering, call 1-800-959-5525.

Register a separate account for a branch or division

To register a separate GST/HST program account for a branch or division of your head office, use Form GST10 Application or Revocation of the Authorization to File Separate GST/HST Returns and Rebate Applications for Branches or Divisions.

Confirmation of registration

You will receive a GST/HST account number as confirmation that your registration is complete.

Register for My Business Account to manage your program accounts online.

After you register

As a GST/HST registrant, you:

  • charge the GST/HST
  • file a GST/HST return
  • remit (pay) the GST/HST
  • may be eligible to claim input tax credits
