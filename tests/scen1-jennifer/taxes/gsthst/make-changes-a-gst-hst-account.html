Options that may be available to you

You can change certain account options to make it is easier to manage your business taxes in a way that makes sense for you. These are called elections. Each election has its own eligibility criteria.

Add separate program accounts

Add separate program accounts

Although you have to register your business as a single entity, you can apply to have your branches or divisions file their own returns.

You may want to keep track of your GST/HST accounts separately if your business has any of the following:

  • more than one location
  • more than one business activity (for example, gas station and convenience store)
  • offices in different provinces
  • divisions
  • different departments (for example, manufacturing and sales)

To be eligible to file separately, your branches or divisions have to be separately identified either by their location or the nature of their activities. They also have to keep separate records. The branches and divisions have to keep the same reporting periods as the parent company.

To register a separate GST/HST program account for a branch or division of your head office, use:

GST10 Application or Revocation of the Authorization to File Separate GST/HST Returns and Rebate Applications for Branches or Divisions

Fiscal year

Fiscal year

You may want to make the following changes to your GST/HST fiscal year:

  • use a calendar fiscal year 
  • use a non-calendar fiscal year
  • change your previously chosen GST/HST fiscal year

You can view or make changes to your GST/HST fiscal year online.

View or make changes online

To make these changes use:

Form GST70, Election or Revocation of an Election to Change a GST/HST Fiscal Year

Reporting period

Reporting period

You have to send us completed GST/HSt returns for each of your reporting periods. That reporting period is usually based on your annual revenue from taxable supplies of property and services made in Canada in either:

  • the immediately preceding fiscal year
  • all preceding fiscal quarters ending in a fiscal year

To make an election to change your reporting period, you have to see if the period you choose is an option available to you.

This chart shows the assigned reporting periods based on your revenues and the options available.

Optional reporting period
Annual Taxable Supplies Assigned reporting period Optional reporting period
$1,500,000 or less Annual Monthly or Quarterly
More than $1,500,000 up to $6,000,000 Quarterly Monthly
More than $6,000,000 Monthly Nil
Charities Annual Monthly or Quarterly
Listed Financial Institutions (except if deemed to be a listed financial institution) Annual Monthly or Quarterly
Calculating your total revenue

When calculating annual taxable supplies include:

  • zero-rated supplies of property and services
  • taxable supplies of all your associates

When calculating annual taxable supplies do not include:

  • supplies made outside Canada
  • zero-rated exports of property and services
  • zero-rated supplies of financial services
  • exempt supplies
  • taxable sales of capital real property
  • supplies of goodwill

If you qualify, you can view or make changes to your GST/HST reporting period online.

View or make changes online

To make these changes use:

Form GST20, Election for GST/HST Reporting Period

If you are a listed financial institution, and you wish to revoke a previously made election to file monthly or quarterly, use:

Form GST20-1, Notice of Revocation of an Election for GST/HST Reporting Period by a Listed Financial Institution

Accounting periods

Accounting periods

You may want to make changes to your GST/HST accounting periods.

You have to notify us that your businesses' accounting periods:

  • vary in length throughout your fiscal year
  • quarter-end and month-end dates do not end on the last day of a calendar month

You can also request approval to use fiscal months that are not within standard guidelines.

You may view or make changes to your accounting periods online.

View or make changes online

To make these changes use:

Form GST71, Notification of Accounting Periods for GST/HST

Important information

You have to file a new notification for each fiscal year for which you want to use your accounting periods. If you do not tell us your accounting periods, we will revert your GST/HST return and payment due dates to calendar months or calendar quarters, as appropriate.

Branches or divisions

Branches or divisions

If you are a public service body, you may be eligible to designate your branch or division to be a small supplier for GST/HST purposes. The branch or division would be treated as a separate person that is a small supplier. Your organization would not collect GST/HST on taxable supplies made through the small supplier division, and it would not be entitled to claim input tax credits for purchases made in relation to the small supplier division.

To set this up, use:

GST31 Application by a Public Service Body to Have Branches or Divisions Designated as Eligible Small Supplier Divisions
