Registering a sole proprietorship or partnership

A sole proprietor or partnership may need to register with the federal government and the provinces and territories where they'll do business. They may also need to apply for permits and licences.

On this page:

  • Registering with provinces or territories
  • Registering with the federal government
  • Applying for permits and licences

Registering with provinces or territories

Most businesses need to register with the provinces and territories where they plan to do business. Sole proprietorshops operating under the name of the business owner often don't need to register. See the website of your provincial or territorial business registrar for more information on their requirements.

(These links to provincial websites may not include all of the registration requirements in all provinces and territories. Please check with the authorities in your provincial or territorial government to determine if there is anything else you need to do.)

(Newfoundland and Labrador does not require provincial registration for sole proprietorships or partnerships.)

Registering with the federal government

Your business may need a federal business number and related CRA program accounts. A sole proprietorship or partnership must register with the federal government if it:

  • has employees
  • charges GST/HST
  • imports or exports goods
