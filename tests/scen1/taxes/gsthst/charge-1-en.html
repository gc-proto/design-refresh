Charge and collect the GST/HST: Which rate to charge

  • 1. Which rate to charge
  • 2. Receipts and invoices
  • 3. What to do with collected GST/HST

Which rate to charge within your province or territory

If you supply a taxable product or service within your province or territory, charge the current rate for your province or territory. However, if you supply a taxable product outside your province or territory, you must determine which rate to charge.

Some exemptions may apply when a supply is made to:

  • Governments and diplomats
  • Charities
  • Municipalities
  • Indigenous peoples

If you are unsure if your product or service is taxable, zero-rated, or exempt, consult Type of supply.

Which rate to charge outside your province or territory

The rate you charge on taxable products or services depends on where the transaction happens. This is called "place of supply".

If you are supplying a product or service:

  • outside your province or territory, go to Place of supply rules
  • outside Canada, go to GST/HST - Import and exports

Current rates

The current rates (as of October 1, 2016) are the following:

  • 5% (GST) in Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, Northwest Territories, Nunavut, Quebec, Saskatchewan, and Yukon
  • 13% (HST) in Ontario
  • 15% (HST) in New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, and Prince Edward Island

How to calculate the GST/HST

In most cases you can use the GST/HST calculator to calculate the GST/HST. In provinces where you have to charge the GST and the provincial sales tax (PST), calculate the GST on the price excluding the PST.

GST/HST in special cases explains cases where the GST/HST is applied differently; for example coupons, gift certificates, and deposits.
